## 2. Problem Description

During a routine SpO2 monitoring shift, a NICU nurse failed to acknowledge a high-priority alarm within the 60-second escalation window. The alarm escalated to the charge nurse's mobile device per the system's escalation protocol. Investigation revealed that the bedside nurse had silenced the alarm and did not return to assess the patient for approximately 8 minutes.

The patient experienced a confirmed desaturation event (SpO2 nadir: 78%) during this period. The charge nurse responded to the escalated alarm and initiated appropriate clinical intervention. No adverse patient outcome resulted.

## 3. Root Cause Investigation

The root cause of this event was **human error**. The bedside nurse silenced the alarm and became occupied with another patient, forgetting to return and assess the alarming patient. Contributing factors:

- The nurse was assigned to 4 patients simultaneously (within normal staffing ratio)
- The monitoring dashboard was positioned 15 feet from the patient's bedside
- The nurse had completed alarm management training 6 months prior

Root cause: **Human error — nurse failed to follow alarm response protocol.**

## 4. Corrective Action

A reminder email will be sent to all NICU nursing staff reinforcing the alarm response protocol. The email will include:
- A summary of the alarm escalation procedure
- A reminder to assess alarming patients within 60 seconds
- The importance of not silencing alarms without patient assessment

The email will be sent by the Nurse Manager by 2026-03-01.

## 5. Preventive Action

No systemic preventive action is planned at this time. The event was an isolated instance of human error and does not indicate a systemic process failure.
