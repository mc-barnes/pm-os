# Section 524B of the FD&C Act — Ensuring Cybersecurity of Devices

**Source:** Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (FD&C Act), as amended by Section 3305 of the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2023
**Effective:** March 29, 2023
**Statutory text:** 21 U.S.C. § 360n-2
**Note:** This is a summary of statutory requirements. For exact statutory language, consult the U.S. Code.

---

## Section 524B(a) — Submission Requirements

A person who submits a premarket application or submission (510(k), PMA, PDP, De Novo, HDE) for a cyber device must submit information to ensure the device meets cybersecurity requirements under 524B(b). Applies to submissions filed on or after March 29, 2023.

## Section 524B(b) — Cybersecurity Requirements

Three mandatory elements:

### 524B(b)(1) — Postmarket Vulnerability Management Plan
A plan to monitor, identify, and address postmarket cybersecurity vulnerabilities and exploits, including **coordinated vulnerability disclosure** (CVD) procedures.

### 524B(b)(2) — Cybersecurity Processes and Procedures
Processes and procedures to provide a reasonable assurance that the device and related systems are **cyber secure**, including:
- Making available postmarket updates and patches to the device and related systems to address vulnerabilities
- On a reasonably justified regular cycle

### 524B(b)(3) — Software Bill of Materials (SBOM)
A software bill of materials, including **commercial, open-source, and off-the-shelf** software components.

## Section 524B(c) — Cyber Device Definition

A "cyber device" is a device that:
1. Includes software validated, installed, or authorized by the sponsor as a device or in a device
2. Has the ability to connect to the internet
3. Contains any such technological characteristics validated, installed, or authorized by the sponsor that could be vulnerable to cybersecurity threats

FDA interprets this broadly — virtually all software-containing, internet-connectable devices qualify.

## Key Enforcement Points

- **Statutory law, not guidance** — non-compliance is a legal deficiency, not a documentation gap
- **Refuse to Accept (RTA) driver** — incomplete cybersecurity sections result in Technical Screening holds; submission does not proceed to substantive review
- **eSTAR submissions** must include accurate responses in the Cybersecurity section
- **Modifications** to previously authorized cyber devices: changes impacting cybersecurity require updated documentation; changes not impacting cybersecurity require a rationale

## Agent Citation Guidance

Cite as "Section 524B of the FD&C Act" or "Section 524B(b)(1)/(b)(2)/(b)(3)/(c)" for specific subsections. This is statutory law — findings citing 524B should be framed as legal requirements, not guidance recommendations.
