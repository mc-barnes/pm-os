# AAMI TIR57:2016 — Principles for Medical Device Security: Risk Management

**Source:** Association for the Advancement of Medical Instrumentation (AAMI)
**Standard:** AAMI TIR57:2016 (Technical Information Report)
**Status:** Published 2016; current edition
**Note:** Copyrighted standard — full text not reproduced. This stub documents scope, structure, and key clauses for agent reference.

## Scope

Provides guidance on performing security risk management for medical devices. Bridges cybersecurity threat analysis with ISO 14971 risk management by connecting cybersecurity threats to patient safety outcomes.

## Key Relationship

AAMI TIR57 is the primary framework for **medical device security risk management** that integrates with ISO 14971. The FDA Premarket Cybersecurity Guidance (June 2025) references TIR57 as a recognized methodology for threat modeling and security risk assessment.

## Key Principles (for agent review reference)

1. **Security risk management must connect to patient safety** — cybersecurity threats are not just IT risks; they must be traced to potential patient harm through the ISO 14971 hazard → hazardous situation → harm chain.
2. **Threat modeling must be systematic** — use recognized methodologies (STRIDE, PASTA, attack trees), not ad-hoc brainstorming.
3. **Exploitability and severity are assessed separately** — consistent with the FDA guidance's approach to cybersecurity risk assessment.
4. **Security risk controls follow the ISO 14971 hierarchy** — inherently safe design → protective measures → information for safety.
5. **Residual security risk must be documented** — with controlled/uncontrolled determination and compensating controls.
6. **Post-production monitoring is required** — vulnerability monitoring, incident response, and risk management updates.
